Khyber Pakhtunkhwa

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, commonly abbreviated as KP or KPK, is one of the four provinces of Pakistan.

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK)


Dresses


The traditional dress of Peshawar and other parts of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan is the khalqa (gown) which opens at the front or shirt which does not open at the front, and the Peshawari shalwar which is very loose down to the ankles.

Foods


KPK is famous for Pashtun style cuisine. Pashtun cuisine is largely based on a plethora of meat dishes that include lamb, beef, chicken, and fresh fish as well as rice and some other vegetables. Accompanying these staples are also dairy products (yogurt, whey, cheeses), various nuts, as well as fresh and dried fruits

Tikka is local cuisine that is BBQ of mutton specially of Sheep.. This is available throughout the City of Peshawar and many other cities of Pakistan but Charsi Tikka is recognised specialist of this cuisine.

Famous Places


Mahabat Khan

The Mahabat Khan Mosque, sometimes spelt Mohabbat Khan Mosque, is a 17th-century Mughal-era mosque in Peshawar, Pakistan. The mosque was built in 1630, and named after the Mughal governor of Peshawar, Nawab Mahabat Khan bin Ali Mardan Khan, known alternatively as Mahabat Khan and Ali Mardan Khan

West of Chowk Yadgar is Mahabat Khan Mosque, the city's finest mosque, built in 1630 by the governor of Peshawar under Mughal emperor Shah Jahan, and renovated in 1898.

Music and Dance


Rubab

The two distinct and most common genres are the Classical and Traditional. They both retain basic Pashto style but level of sophistication varies. Traditional folk Pashto songs can be performed using only a single instrument.

Pashto songs are usually very short (1 to 3 lines). The first lines are repeated in the middle of the song and Tappa is usually added according to the subject and circumstances. Most of these songs in Pashtun culture have been expressed in different areas about daily life and love.
